What is a school and what is it for?

Extrinsic v Intrinsic Rewards

Broadly, there are two types of "rewards" or reasons teachers are teachers: Extrinsic, aka external rewards, and instrinic, or internal rewards

    Extrinsic
  • Salary
  • Higher status (everyone needs to be taught, and teaching is very noble)
  • Power
  • Work Schedule (summer's off!)
    Intrinsic
  • Enjoyable profession / teaching in of itself
  • Teaching is for the better good
  • Knowing other teachers
  • Joy of the process

School v Education

How do education and school relate?

Education is an ever-changing, active, random process. Everything you do teaches you something

Schools are places intended to systematize this process. Schools are passive, preplanned experiences to teach you the basics needed in life and work.

What are some parts of a school?

Schools are much more than the learning. Schools have entire cultures, aka beliefs, ideas, stories, art, habits, organization, language, and more!

A school culture can be positive or negative, and it's very important for school officials to work to better the culture. This is based in every interaction between student and staff.

School cultures typically try to meet a goal, laid out by the Mission statement. Almost every school has one, and even businesses or organizations often have them.

What are some types of teachers?

If a school has a culture, so does a classroom. These environs are set up by the type of teacher one is. One way to catogorize teachers is through what type of reconstructionist they are

  • Social Reconstructioniss believe in empowering students to be agents of change.
  • Democratic Reconstructioniss believe in setting the responcibility of the students to solve the issues of the world.
  • Economic Reconstructioniss believe in a harder view of the dominent culture and see schools has servants to those in power, and wish to change that
